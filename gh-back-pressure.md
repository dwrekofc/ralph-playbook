# don’t waste your back pressure

![Image 3: don’t waste your back pressure](https://ghuntley.com/content/images/size/w1200/2026/01/Bright-lens-flare-with-warm-terracotta-and-cool-teal-tones--a-symbolic-traditional-tattoo-art-print-of-a-stalled-wheel-and-AI-robot--in-vibrant-colors--retro-flair--complex-ornamental-style-on-a-white-background--showcasi.jpg)

I am fortunate to be surrounded by folks who listen and the link below post will go down as a seminal reading for people interested in AI context engineering.

A simple convo between mates - well Moss translated it into words and i’ve been waiting for it to come out so I didn’t front run him.

[Don’t waste your back pressure · Back pressure for agents You might notice a pattern in the most successful applications of agents over the last year. Projects that are able to setup structure around the agent itself, to provide it with automated feedback on quality and correctness, have been able to push them to work on longer horizon tasks. This back pressure helps the agent identify mistakes as it progresses and models are now good enough that this feedback can keep them aligned to a task for much longer. As an engineer, this means you can increase your leverage by delegating progressively more complex tasks to agents, while increasing trust that when completed they are at a satisfactory standard. ![Image 4](https://ghuntley.com/content/images/icon/favicon-8.ico)[Moss] ![Image 5](https://ghuntley.com/content/images/thumbnail/plot_0.png)](https://banay.me/dont-waste-your-backpressure/?ref=ghuntley.com)

read this and internalise this

Enjoy. This is what engineering now looks like in the post loom/gastown era or even when doing ralph loops.

![Image 6](https://ghuntley.com/content/images/2026/01/IMG_0614.jpeg)

software engineering is now about preventing failure scenarios and preventing the wheel from turning over through back pressure to the generative function

If you aren’t capturing your back-pressure then you are failing as a software engineer.
